Customisation for the Dyson Airwrap™

Role
UX & UI Designer
Company
Dyson
Focus
Customisation · E-commerce
Methods
A/B testing · Card sorting

Overview

A flagship tool for a flagship product

I led the end-to-end design of Dyson's flagship Airwrap Customisation Tool — an interactive experience enhancing product discovery, conversion, and personalisation. The goal was to make a highly technical, premium product feel intuitive and tailored for first-time users.

As design lead, I drove a collaborative, agile process — partnering closely with engineers, researchers, and global stakeholders to continuously align evolving design decisions with core business objectives and accessibility standards. Through iterative prototyping and cross-device validation, we refined a guided flow that helped users build their ideal Airwrap set based on hair type and styling needs.

Research & Testing

Two themes, tested head-to-head

I led the research and testing strategy, ensuring the experience resonated across audiences without overwhelming users. We explored multiple visual and functional directions before refining two distinct themes: the bold, expressive "Angel of the North" (inspired by British design language) and a clean, minimalist Apple-inspired aesthetic.

Working closely with product, engineering, content, and accessibility teams, I facilitated cross-functional workshops to align on hypotheses, success metrics, and user goals. Using high-fidelity Figma prototypes, I designed interactive flows for in-person usability sessions, enabling participants to explore both customisation concepts in realistic contexts.

Through A/B testing, card-sorting exercises, and mixed-method studies, we evaluated how effectively users could navigate, understand, and complete customisation tasks. Users managing more complex configurations preferred the Angel of the North theme for its stronger hierarchy and clearer visual affordances, while microinteractions enhanced engagement and perceived responsiveness.

Outcomes

Key takeaways

  • Users spent significantly more time engaging with the tool, indicating higher interest and usability.
  • Bounce rate dropped due to improved storytelling and guidance through the flow.
  • The new tool aligned better with user expectations and styling needs, reflected in satisfaction scores and completion rates.

Challenges

Designing within real constraints

I encountered significant accessibility and platform limitations that shaped how far we could push the experience. These constraints required balancing design ambition with technical feasibility — simplifying certain interactions or rethinking workflows to maintain consistency across Dyson's infrastructure.

A proposed live support feature inspired by Apple's expert chat had to be deprioritised due to technical restrictions — prompting us to guide users through contextual prompts and support touchpoints instead.

As the project evolved, shifting business priorities and late-stage feature requests demanded a highly agile approach. One key adaptation was introducing a dedicated customisation step between the product page and basket — preserving technical compatibility while creating a more seamless, user-focused flow.

To ensure design quality across global teams, I developed a comprehensive design specification document detailing interaction patterns, layout behaviours, and accessibility requirements for each platform — bridging business objectives with genuine user value.
