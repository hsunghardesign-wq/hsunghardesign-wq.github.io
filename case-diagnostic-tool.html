The problem

A helpful tool nobody could find

The Help Me Choose tool was a project I led end-to-end, beginning with heatmaps, analytics, and engagement metrics to identify where users were dropping off and why engagement was low.

We discovered users struggled to locate the tool — buried at level 3 in the site's information architecture — and once inside, the journey was perceived as overly complex and cognitively demanding. The tool was designed to serve a dual purpose — guiding product selection while educating users — but instead it either overloaded people with too much detail or provided too little context, undermining confidence and leading to abandonment.

The approach

Progressive disclosure, tested with users

I first redesigned the entry point, moving it to a higher-level category page to surface the tool earlier in the journey. I then reviewed the question flow and content hierarchy so information was presented progressively — reducing cognitive load and creating a more guided experience. Using card sorting, I identified what context users valued most and structured the flow accordingly.

From there, I ran A/B testing with 20 participants: one variant used simple imagery and straightforward questions; the other integrated contextual "Q tips" revealing additional information on demand.

12/20valued Q tips for added clarity — but sometimes found them overwhelming
8/20preferred the simpler version
The results revealed the need for a hybrid approach — balancing clarity with optional depth.

Constraints

Creativity within platform limits

A key challenge was working within platform limitations. The advanced logic underpinning the tool meant we couldn't integrate richer formats such as videos or carousels. The only feasible enhancement was Q tips — a lightweight, flexible mechanism for providing context without disrupting the tool's core logic.

While this constraint restricted the design space, it forced creative thinking about micro-interactions, copy, and progressive disclosure to deliver the right level of support at the right time.

The redesigned tool balanced guidance with simplicity, reduced cognitive load, and increased user confidence in product choices. The final experience is now live, providing a more intuitive product-selection journey that aligns user needs with business objectives.
