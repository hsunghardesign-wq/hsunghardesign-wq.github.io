Dyson Supersonic r™ Campaign

Role
Project Lead · UX
Company
Dyson
Focus
Launch campaign · PDP
Impact
+5% PDP engagement

Overview

Full ownership, start to finish

The Supersonic r campaign was an incredible project to lead and one I took full ownership of from start to finish. This was the first major release of the new Supersonic r, and my involvement began by defining the core product claims we wanted to communicate: ergonomic design, the most powerful handheld performance, and lightweight build.

Once these key claims were established, I developed a comprehensive user journey — mapping all potential entry points including email campaigns, third-party advertising, social media, display ads, and Google search. I tailored the messaging for both new customers and existing Dyson owners, ensuring the communication was relevant and impactful for each audience segment.

After finalising the messaging, I audited all entry points within the Dyson ecosystem to understand how users would navigate from the homepage to the product detail page (PDP). This included a full content mapping exercise and identifying optimal signposting opportunities across the site — ensuring users had enough information and confidence before reaching the PDP.

The challenge

Claims buried below the fold

One critical challenge was ensuring the key product claims were visible early in the PDP journey. Initially, these claims were only accessible if users scrolled — risking them being missed entirely.

To resolve this, I introduced "category cards" — modular elements replacing traditional anchor links. These cards highlighted the product's main benefits upfront and acted as interactive modules anchoring users directly to relevant sections of the page.
+5%increase in user engagement with the PDP
Adoptedacross multiple Dyson PDPs as a new standard

Validation

Testing before scaling

Implementing category cards wasn't as simple as placing them into the layout — it required rigorous testing phases and template adjustments. I was responsible for validating the effectiveness of this addition, ensuring it aligned with both user needs and business objectives.

I led usability testing sessions and data analysis to evaluate user interaction. Results clearly showed users engaged with the cards more frequently and used them to navigate the PDP more efficiently — making a strong case for their integration.

Once the value was proven, I oversaw updating the PDP design system to incorporate category cards as a standard module — establishing a scalable pattern reused across future product pages.
